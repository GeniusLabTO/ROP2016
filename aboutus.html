About Us

The transition from adolescence to responsible adulthood is possibly the most tumultuous passage we, as humans, will undergo. It is filled with emotional, cognitive and physiological challenges, that often result in questioning, rebellion and uncertainty.

Rites of Passage is not a “program”, it is a process of maturation and personal development. Just like similar practices in many indigenous cultures, it is designed to assist young people as they transition into responsible adulthood. This process has a long-term positive impact on one's values, perception of self, level of discipline and commitment to community. Rites, known by many names, is practiced in First Nations Cultures of North and South America, the original Peoples of Australia, and in many Afrikan traditional cultures, just to name a few.

The Akan of West Afrika believe that this process is critical in affirming and clarifying four age old questions, which, if successfully instituted and embraced, will harvest young adults poised to contribute to the continued building and sustainability of their communities.

  • Who am I?
  • How did I come to be who I am?
  • Am I really who I think I am?
  • What is my life purpose?

The National Rites of Passage Institute (NROPI) in collaboration with communities, organizations, practitioners and scholars, has been elevating the value of culturally-based Rites of Passage in youth and community development for more than 30 years.

The impact of racism, enslavement and the subsequent weakening of cultural systems has devastating and lasting impact on the socialization practice of young people of Afrikan descent to this day. For example, low levels of educational attainment, high unemployment and engagement in high risk behaviors are all a result of the weakened social systems. Knowing where one comes from and the contributions one’s people have made to the development of human history gives one cultural, personal and reverent power.
